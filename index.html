About

I am a GIS and geospatial research analyst with over four years of professional experience across government and university settings. My work centres on applying spatial analysis and geospatial technologies to support evidence-based understanding of environmental systems and place-based decision-making. I have worked collaboratively with multidisciplinary teams on innovative projects, contributing to the development and automation of geospatial workflows that enhance efficiency, data integrity, and reproducibility. I enjoy solving complex problems through spatial reasoning—whether by integrating diverse datasets, improving analytical processes, or communicating results to both technical and non-technical stakeholders. Alongside my technical background, I have a strong interest in the legal and governance dimensions of environmental and spatial decision-making. This perspective allows me to examine how environmental laws, planning frameworks, and policy instruments intersect with spatial data, land use, and environmental change. I see geospatial analysis as a critical bridge between environmental evidence and legal or regulatory action. My experience also includes fieldwork, which grounds my analytical work in real-world conditions and strengthens my appreciation of data quality, scale, and context. Overall, my approach combines geospatial analysis, environmental research, and legal awareness to better understand and address complex spatial and environmental challenges.
